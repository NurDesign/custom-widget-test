The Client and Their Business

The client ran a mid-sized clothing brand based in the UK, selling physical apparel and limited edition drops to a growing online audience. Business was picking up. The audience was there. But their website had no store, no cart, no checkout. Every order was being handled through DMs and emails, one by one.

The Problem

Customers were coming to the site ready to buy and leaving because there was no way to actually do it. Every sale required manual back and forth which was costing the client hours every day and costing the business real money. They needed a proper ecommerce store built from the ground up, one that matched their brand and could handle real volume.

My Solution and Approach

I chose WooCommerce because the client was already on WordPress and needed full design control. Moving to a new platform entirely would have cost more time and money than the problem was worth. Before any development started I mapped out the full product structure, payment flow, and order system so there were no surprises mid build.

Challenges I Solved

Variable Products

The client sold clothing in multiple sizes and colors, each with their own stock levels. Getting this to work cleanly in WooCommerce without the UI breaking or stock miscounting took careful setup and a fair amount of custom CSS work.

Payment Gateway Conflicts

The client wanted both Stripe and PayPal running at the same time. They were conflicting in the checkout and causing sessions to drop. I debugged it until both gateways worked without any friction for the customer.

Site Speed

The product image gallery was heavy and the site was slow because of it. I compressed everything, set up lazy loading, and configured caching properly. The store ended up fast enough to handle traffic spikes without slowing down.
